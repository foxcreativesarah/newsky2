How to find Cetus

Cetus the Whale can be found in the southern sky during the winter. It is the fourth largest constellation, so look for it stretching out across the sky. Finding it might be a bit tricky. First look for three bright stars in a row. These stars are Orion’s Belt. Measure 20º west with your hand, from Orion’s belt to the bright star Alderbaran the ‘Bull’s Eye’ in the constellation, Taurus the Bull. Measure another 30º west with your hand and you will find the stars that make up Cetus the Whale’s head.

Cetus' Story

Cetus was described by the ancient Greeks as a sea monster, who Poseidon the Sea God used to punish misbehaving Greek kings and queens.
